Understanding the Election Process

Voting is your voice in a democracy. Join us on an interactive journey to learn how elections work—from registration to the final count, step by step.

What are Elections?

An election is a formal decision-making process by which a population chooses an individual or multiple individuals to hold public office. It's the core mechanism of a democracy.

🏛️

Choosing Leaders

Citizens vote to choose representatives who will make laws and decisions on their behalf.

🗣️

Having a Voice

It ensures that the government reflects the will of the people. Your vote is your voice.

⚖️

Accountability

Elections keep leaders accountable. If citizens aren't happy, they can vote for someone else next time.

The Election Journey

Follow the steps from becoming an eligible voter to seeing the final results.

1

Voter Registration

Timeline: Months before the election

Before you can vote, you must register. This ensures you are eligible and assigns you to the correct voting district.

Example: Think of it like signing up for a membership. You need to prove who you are (e.g., ID or utility bill) and where you live before you can participate.
2

Campaigning

Timeline: Weeks to Months before

Candidates share their ideas, debate issues, and try to convince you to vote for them through rallies, ads, and town halls.

3

Election Day (Voting)

Timeline: The Big Day!

You visit a polling station (or mail in a ballot), review the candidates, and secretly cast your vote. This is the main event!

Example: You go to a local school or community center, get a piece of paper (a ballot), fill in a bubble next to your preferred candidate, and drop it in a sealed box.
4

Counting & Results

Timeline: Election night to a few days after

Polls close, and officials begin counting the ballots. Depending on the system, results might be announced late at night or days later.

Example: Like counting hands raised in a classroom, but on a massive scale using secure machines and verified by multiple officials.
